Corresponding Adjustments (Entsprechende Anpassungen)

  • Host country adds back transferred emissions to its NDC accounting — prevents double counting
  • Buyer country subtracts the ITMOs from its own target — clean claim
  • Without CA: credits are VCM-only, no compliance use, no CORSIA eligibility

Price impact: VCM without CA = USD 5-10/t → With CA (ITMO) = USD 20+/t (2-3x premium)

DRC Gap GAP

  • No CH-DRC Art. 6.2 treaty — DRC not SECO priority country
  • VCM-only pathway = USD 5-10/t (no corresponding adjustment)
  • With Art. 6.2: potential USD 20+/t — survival strategy for SPV economics
  • 50/25/25 benefit-sharing waterfall makes VCM pricing near-unviable
  • Strategic lever: Initiate CH-DRC Art. 6.2 treaty via BAFU/SECO channels

Contract Checklist — Art. 6 Readiness

  • Dual-pathway clause — VCM + Art. 6.2/ITMO optionality in every credit contract
  • Pathway-differentiated revenue — separate pricing for VCM vs. ITMO credits
  • Host Country Authorization as CP — Letter of Authorization (LoA) as condition precedent
  • Sovereign risk provisions — what happens if host country revokes CA or changes NDC
  • Registry compatibility check — ensure project registry supports ITMO transfers
