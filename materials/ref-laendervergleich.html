DRC: Why Conditional?

5 Showstoppers

  • Sanctions: OFAC/EU/SECO/UN all active; RDF sanctioned 02.03.2026; 50%-rule
  • Corruption: CPI 20, Glencore precedent (CHF 2M + USD 150M fine)
  • Regulatory vacuum: ARMCA: 6 of 9 implementing regulations missing
  • Security: M23 controls Goma + Bukavu; 7.3M displaced; infrastructure 19% electrification
  • No DBA: 14% WHT gross-basis; Belgian/SA competitors pay 0% via treaty

Indonesia: Why GO?

  • Perpres 110/2025: Moratorium lifted, carbon trade before NDC targets allowed
  • 5 MRAs: Verra, Gold Standard, Plan Vivo, ACR, Social Carbon recognized (Art. 66)
  • 4 Art. 6.2 bilaterals: Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Norway (CH in preparation)
  • CORSIA: Mandatory from Jan 2026, accepts eligible VCM credits
  • DBA advantage: WHT only 5% on service fees (vs. 20% domestic rate)

Malaysia: Client Jurisdiction

Not a project site but a client jurisdiction. Key structural facts:
  • Sdn Bhd (Sendirian Berhad) = standard private company form
  • MACC Sec. 17A: Corporate liability for corruption (Angemessenheitspruefung / adequate procedures defense)
  • FSI taxation: Labuan / offshore structures, 0% WHT possible
  • Non-compete VOID: Sec. 28 Contracts Act 1950 — unenforceable by law
  • Bursa Carbon Exchange: Regulated by Securities Commission Malaysia (since Dec 2022)

Bolivia: Emerging

  • Ban overturned: June 2024, carbon markets previously prohibited
  • Laconic deal: USD 1.2B carbon credit agreement announced
  • RENAPP: National registry launched March 2025
  • No framework yet: No carbon tax, no DBA with CH, no Art. 6.2
  • High uncertainty: Regulatory direction unclear, political risk elevated
